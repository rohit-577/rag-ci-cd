Elephants are the largest land animals on Earth. There are three species: the African bush elephant, the African forest elephant, and the Asian elephant. African elephants can weigh up to 6000 kilograms. Elephants have the longest gestation period of any mammal at 22 months. They are known for their intelligence, strong social bonds, and excellent memory. An elephant's trunk contains about 40000 muscles. The global elephant population has declined by over 60 percent in the last 50 years.
