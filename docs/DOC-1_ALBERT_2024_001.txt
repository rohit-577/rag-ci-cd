Albert Einstein was born on March 14, 1879 in Ulm, Germany. He developed the theory of relativity, one of the two pillars of modern physics. His famous equation E=mc² demonstrates the relationship between energy and mass. Einstein won the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921 for his explanation of the photoelectric effect. He died on April 18, 1955 in Princeton, New Jersey. Einstein's IQ was estimated to be around 160.
