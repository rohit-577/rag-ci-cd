The Nile River is the longest river in the world, stretching approximately 6650 kilometers through northeastern Africa. It flows through 11 countries including Egypt, Sudan, and Uganda. The Blue Nile and the White Nile are its two main tributaries. Ancient Egyptian civilization developed along the banks of the Nile. The river provides water for irrigation and hydroelectric power. The Aswan High Dam, completed in 1970, controls flooding and generates electricity.
