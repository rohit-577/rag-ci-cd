The blue whale is the largest animal on Earth, reaching lengths of up to 30 meters and weighing as much as 200 tons. Its heart alone can weigh as much as a small car. Blue whales can live for 80 to 90 years. They consume up to 4 tons of krill each day. Despite their enormous size, blue whales are filter feeders. Their populations were severely reduced by whaling in the 20th century and they are now classified as endangered species.
